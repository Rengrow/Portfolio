Gamusinos Fantasy

General Description

Gamusinos Fantasy is a turn-based RPG that utilizes GPS locations to complete missions in real-world settings, encouraging players to venture outdoors.

Setting

The game's setting is based on a fantastical and medieval world with futuristic elements, where all enemies are wordplay-based.

Story

Players assume the role of an adventurer aiming to defeat the most powerful creature in the universe, the Gamusino, which resides just below the gods. Before facing it, players must become stronger by confronting enemies in various missions unlocked as they grow more powerful.

Distinctive Game Features:

The game consists of two parts, each handling specific user tasks.

Desktop Application
Manage the character's attributes. Handle equipment (weapons and armor). Manage the account (account creation, residency management for missions, password changes). Handle the inventory and transfer rewards obtained from the mobile app. Select missions to complete on the mobile app.
Mobile Application
Complete selected missions. Engage in battles (both random encounters and mission enemies). Obtain new resources.

Visual Style

The game adopts a cartoon-style aesthetic, with enemies satirized based on their respective wordplay. Despite this, they will become increasingly threatening as players progress in the game.

Scope

The goal is to develop a closed, robust, and flexible game that can be expanded in the future without excessive complications. Initially, basic content will be created due to time constraints and project complexity.

Mechanics

This section aims to delve into the mechanics of Gamusinos Fantasy, covering all aspects of gameplay. It specifies the actions players can take in different environments (desktop and mobile) and provides a list of enemies, weapons, armor, and items.

Gameplay

Creation and Improvement: Players can create and enhance their equipment, both weapons and armor, experiencing the improvement of their character. Player Progression: As the game advances, players can increase their attributes through points earned by fighting enemies. Difficulty: As players enhance their characteristics, they'll notice enemies becoming stronger and posing a greater challenge. Backpack Loss: In the event of death in combat, all items in the player's backpack will be lost.

Levels

Players can confront enemies in two ways: through missions or random encounters. When facing a mission enemy, it will have a predefined level, requiring a necessary level to defeat it. This establishes the difficulty curve. If encountering a random enemy, it will be three levels above or below the player's current level, ensuring a balanced challenge.

Gameplay Flow

The player initiates Gamusinos Fantasy on the computer and is presented with the main menu, containing the following elements:

Home, where the player's character with current attributes is displayed, and upgrading costs are shown. Here, the player can also change weapons and armor. Chest, displaying weapons, armor, and items. Rewards obtained in the mobile app can be claimed here. Anvil, showcasing all weapons and armor in the game, including their features. If the necessary items are possessed, players can create and add them to their inventory. Mission Board, where players can select one or multiple missions, displaying those available for their current rank. Frequently Asked Questions section to address potential queries or obtain information about the game.

On the mobile device, once the player logs in, they encounter an exploration screen with a button to generate random enemy encounters and check mission zones accepted on the desktop.

A map displays the locations of accepted missions.A side button offers three options:

Bestiary: Discover more information about defeated enemies.
Ranking: View the Top 10 players with the highest levels in the game.
FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions section similar to the desktop version to address queries or obtain information.

The bottom menu has four options:

Exploration screen (default upon opening the application).
Missions screen to review accepted missions
Backpack screen to store items obtained from enemies (later claimed on the desktop)

Combat

Upon entering combat, the enemy appears vertically at the end of the screen, facing the player. The player has three options: attack, defend, and escape. The attribute of speed determines who attacks first. Doubling the enemy's speed results in a double attack. Damage dealt to the enemy is determined by the player's strength and the enemy's resistance. Choosing to defend reduces damage based on the player's resistance and the enemy's strength.

After defeating the enemy, the luck attribute influences the quantity of items received. Choosing to escape ends the combat, returning to the navigation screen.
