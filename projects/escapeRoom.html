Design of a Escape Room

Setting

For the design of this escape room, I aimed to convey the idea of "Living an experience" (Miguel Ángel and Susana (Escape Barcelona), p.9). Therefore, in the escape room design, I have imposed some limitations on myself since H.P. Lovecraft's stories are set in the 1920s. I intentionally avoided adding any elements post-dating this period. Due to this decision, I refrained from using common escape room elements such as numerical locks or electronic devices, as they would disrupt the immersion I was seeking.

Players will be divided into two groups: the intruders and the guards. The first group will proceed to "question" the old man. The other two will wait outside, similar to the original story, but with the difference that this time our participants will have the chance to survive this adventure.

The intruders will be locked in, and it will be explained to them that they have just awakened and the last thing they remember is knocking on the old man's door. They recall nothing else once the door opened. Therefore, they should hurry to escape before the old man arrives to enact whatever sinister plan he may have in mind.

The players waiting outside will be informed that their companions are taking too long. If they want to check, they should do so discreetly, entering through the back of the property, going through a broken window. As they pass through the hole, the game master will make a stone move to cover the hole they just entered through.

General Details

The escape room is designed for groups of 4 people. All 4 members are briefed that they are a group of unnatural thieves from Kingsport who have come to pay a visit to the old man, searching for the Spanish silver and gold coins undoubtedly hidden somewhere in his decaying abode. In case of needing a hint at any time, we can use a light for signaling or, in a more extreme case, the game master would use a filtered megaphone to sound like a superior entity.

Description of Space

In each room, there may be additional furniture and ambiance elements, but only essential ones for the escape room are shown in this plan. I will now discuss each space individually.

Dungeon

The dungeon is a space reminiscent of a damp basement, where there's little light. The walls are made of old wood with mold creeping out from the corners. The floor is earthen, and in the center of the room, there's a kind of luminous fungus providing a faint glow, except for a small hole in the roof letting in the pale moonlight.

To the left, there's a small, moldy mirror hanging on the wall next to an old, dirty bed. On the right side of the bed, there's a small shelf with books. Just beside it, there's a small hole, roughly eye-sized, with the word "rudder" written above it. On the opposite side of the mirror, there's an illegible text "vi" (inverted horizontally so it must be viewed in the mirror) written on the wall. Adjacent to it is a hole, about 10x7cm, leading to another room. Finally, at the back, there's a barred door with a large padlock on the outside, securely closed.

Stone Chamber

This room is an old office with dilapidated wooden panels, peeling wallpaper, and crumbling ceiling plaster. Filled with stones with various ancient idol shapes, as mentioned in the story (see Illustration 2), the three significant ones are marked on the plan:

The bottom center stone that closes the entrance to the players. The right one, shaped like a face with an open mouth, containing a magnet inside. The top-left one, again a face-shaped stone, but this time with a closed mouth.

In the center of the room on the left, there's a desk with a newspaper clipping:

On the table, hung on the wall, there will be a world map from that era with continental reliefs, a metallic ship, and a frame protruding above the relief, making the map and the ship untouchable (referencing the fact that we are in a port city and the old man was a ship captain).

Transition Hallway

This corridor serves as a transition between the first and second halves of the escape room and is where the players gather. It's a dark and dusty hallway, similar to the office of the stones, with creaky wooden panels, torn wallpaper, and crumbling ceiling plaster. It has old wooden floors that creak as players walk, breaking the silence. There are three doors:

The dungeon door, a barred door with a large padlock. The door leading to the room of the terrible old man, a wooden door with a glass panel from waist to the top, very dirty and dusty. A door blocked by wood, leading to the office of the stones.

Bottle Room

This room resembles what we would know as a dining room, spacious, with exposed beams illuminated only by small skylights at its ends with small cobwebs. Upon entering, a stool is seen in front of a small desk with a bottle with a lead pendulum hanging, along with gold coins and a plant. The old man's gnarled cane leans against the desk. Next to it is a small window resembling a U.S. basement, tall and narrow, with broken glass. In the center of the room is a shelf with a collection of bottles in various shapes, all with a hanging pendulum and labels like "Scarface", "Spanish Joe"...

At the back of the room, in the center, is the exit door with the text above: "Who are you? (In the tablet's language)." In the lower right corner is a three-drawer dresser. The dresser is not against the wall, allowing visibility from behind.

Puzzles and Player Progression

Players discover they are in adjacent rooms and start communicating. The office players read the clue, and the dungeon players see a hole labeled "rudder," where they can observe a specific stone with an open mouth.

With this information, the office players must obtain a magnet inside a face-shaped stone, inserting their hand into the statue's mouth. Meanwhile, in the dungeon, there's a small shelf with old books containing a map of the Atlantic Ocean with a set route and an X, marking the destination of the expedition.

At this point, players in the room will know that the map is a puzzle. But since the map is framed with the ship, they cannot move it. So, they must exchange the map and the magnet through the hole. The office players guide the dungeon players on how to move the ship without seeing the map until they reach the X. Once the point is reached, pressing a button on the ship will open the statue's mouth next door to the door, and they must pass the key to the dungeon players, who will manage to unlock their door.

In the hallway, dungeon players must remove the barricade from the office and see the door at the end. Through it, they will witness an actor portraying the terrible old man talking to a bottle, explaining that there are new members for the group, but he must go fetch his tools. The old man will exit through the door, and the door to the bottle room will unlock.

In this room, the first thing players will see is the desk where the terrible old man was, which has all the elements necessary to overcome the challenges in the room. The bottles will make the sound of "cling," similar to the audiobook (The Terrible Old Man - Illustrated Horror Story by H.P. Lovecraft - Lynx's Tales) from lower to higher frequency to warn them of the remaining time before the terrible old man returns.

When the group starts investigating the room, they will find the dresser. In the first drawer, there will be a pencil. The second will be empty, and the third, being larger than the others, will be stuck and cannot be opened. However, it will have a hole in the upper right corner where they can insert the old man's cane to pull the back and find the scale. Using the coins to balance the scale will open a compartment containing a note that reads: "False bottom."

This is a simple puzzle, as it is a clue puzzle and does not penalize players who find the bottom without the need for the clue.

In the second drawer, there will be a false bottom accessible by inserting the pencil through a hole in the bottom, lifting the bottom, and the players will find the tablet and the translation clue.

With the tablet and the clue on how to use it, players will translate the message above the final door, resolving the final question: "Who am I?"

To solve it, players must return to the previous rooms, where they must unlock the final word by syllables. In the office, being longer, there won't be puzzles, and they should translate it as is. But in the dungeon, they must look through the mirror, as the letters will be horizontally inverted. Then, in the note, they will have the password.

They should approach the door and say the password: "Providence" (referring to Lovecraft's tombstone). Then, the game master will open the door, and they can safely escape. Otherwise, the terrible old man will appear, and they will perish as in the story.
