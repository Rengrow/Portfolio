UX Audit of the Website "Pisos.com"

Heuristic Analysis

I did a first analysis of the website based on the 10 principles of Nielsen. Where I sorted the problems that I found in order of severity.

I will mention an example:


The website lacks a top search bar.

The search bar, which should appear on all sections of the website, only shows up when viewing property listings.

Where the issue occurs:

It happens on various pages of the website: Home page, selection of the housing search area, and map-based search.

Heuristic principle violated:

This error breaches the principle of consistency and standards.

Explanation:

Not only does it break a web design convention by not placing the search bar in the header, which could be justified on the Home page as a central element, but it is also absent on other pages like the mentioned selection of the housing search area. Additionally, the absence of the search bar is not consistent throughout the website, as it appears when viewing property listings. This lack of consistency is not in line with the website's own design.

Screenshot of the problem:
Solution or improvement:

The search bar should be included on all screens, except for the Home page, where it can be presented as a central elemen

Example of a solution:

On Fotocasa, the navigation bar is present on all pages of the website except the Home page.

Heuristic Analysis

Socio-demographic Profiling

To better understand the target audience of "pisos.com," a socio-demographic profiling exercise was conducted.

According to SimilarWeb, the gender distribution of visitors to pisos.com is 53.23% male and 46.77% female. The age distribution reveals that the two most prominent visitor groups are aged 25-34 and 35-44, accounting for 19.78% and 20.68%, respectively.

When we correlate this data with the shared housing study on pisos.com, we can narrow down the profile of individuals seeking rental or shared accommodation. Approximately 51.05% of those seeking shared housing fall within the 18-25 age group, except in the Balearic Islands, where the majority fall within the 26-35 age range.

Consequently, most individuals aged 18-25 may not have the financial capacity for renting. According to the H1 2023 report on buying and selling experiences by Fotocasa, another real estate platform, and confirmed by the Colliers' European Residential on the Rise report in Spain, the average age at which individuals become property owners is 41, evenly split between men and women.

This provides us with the average profile of someone seeking to buy or rent a property. However, there is a third user profile that we haven't addressed until now, as mentioned by Miguel √Ångel Alemany, pisos.com's general director:

Our goal is to build loyalty beyond the property search, creating an online real estate community where professionals and potential buyers can share opinions, generate information, create their own blogs, and share common interests.

This profile corresponds to real estate agents using the platform for their clients. Based on the gathered information, we can assume that this person is well-established, likely a property owner, and falls within an age range consistent with the studies.

Considering the above, we can confidently identify three distinct user profiles:

  1. The user seeking a rental is likely a woman aged 18 to 25.
  2. The user seeking to buy a property is probably a man around 41 years old, likely with a partner and children.
  3. The professional user, utilizing the application for client property searches, is likely a woman over 41 with experience in these types of websites and applications.

Given these three user groups, testing with three individuals from each group seems ideal. While usability tests commonly involve five participants, conducting tests with three individuals from each group should be sufficient and cost-effective, considering the similarities in the tests, which may lead to overlapping issues and insights from the website.

User Testing

Test Design

Three distinct user tests were designed to evaluate different aspects of the website's usability. These tests aimed to assess the effectiveness of key features, navigation pathways, and the overall satisfaction of users interacting with the platform. Test scenarios were carefully crafted to simulate real-world user interactions.

Rental Scenario

You have started discussing with your partner that it would be a good time to live together and test your life as a couple. Therefore, you decide it would be a good idea to look for a rental property in Terrassa.

Tasks:

  1. Could you access the website www.pisos.com and try to find a rental property in the Terrassa area?
    Objective: Check which navigation method users prefer, either the search bar or the area-based search, and see if the website's design contributes to user comfort or distraction.
  2. After browsing through properties, you remember that parking in Terrassa is challenging. Can you search for a rental property with a garage?
    Objective: Confirm that the website's filters and search options are clearly laid out for users.
  3. You've found a couple of interesting options, but the rent is quite high. Can you set up an alert to notify you if the rental price falls within your budget?
    Objective: Ensure that users have easy access to the "notify me if the price drops" option.
  4. Can you check the energy certificate of the property that caught your interest the most?
    Objective: Confirm that property features are easily accessible to users
  5. Can you contact the advertiser of the property you are most interested in?
    Objective: Ensure users have easy access to contact the seller, as it is a critical point in the website's usage cycle.
Sales Scenario

After living with your partner for a while and realizing that cohabitation is going well, you decide it's time to buy a new house. Since you are not in a hurry and have experienced renovations when living with your parents, you think a new construction apartment would be best. Additionally, you have fallen in love with the neighborhood and made new friends, so you wouldn't want to move far from this area.

Tasks:

  1. Could you access the website www.pisos.com and try to find a rental property in the Terrassa area?
    Objective: Check which navigation method users prefer, either the search bar or the area-based search, and see if the website's design contributes to user comfort or distraction.
  2. After browsing through properties, your partner asks if you are looking for one with a pool because they've always wanted a pool. Can you search for a new construction property with a pool?
    Objective: Confirm that the website's filters and search options are clearly laid out for users.
  3. You've found a property that catches your eye, it's in the right area, but it's a bit expensive. Can you view more available properties from the same development?
    Objective: Ensure that users know how to view options within the same building they initially looked at.
  4. Can you check how the mortgage calculation would be with the property's price from the website?
    Objective: Confirm that the functionality of calculating an indicative mortgage is accessible to users.
  5. Can you contact the advertiser of the property you are most interested in?
    Objective: Ensure users have easy access to contact the seller, as it is a critical point in the website's usage cycle.
